THE HISTORY OF UNITED ARAB EMIRATES

Before becoming the United Arab Emirates, the region was home to tribes and coastal communities known for pearl diving, fishing, and trade. With the discovery of oil and the formation of the union in 1971, the Emirates began a journey of rapid transformation. Guided by visionary leadership and a deep respect for heritage, the UAE grew into a modern nation that connects its proud past with a bold, ambitious future.

Trucial States Era

In the 1800s, several tribal sheikhdoms along the Gulf coast signed peace agreements with Britain, forming what became known as the Trucial States. These deals helped stabilize the region and laid early foundations for unity.

Discovery of Oil

In the late 1950s, oil was discovered in Abu Dhabi. By the 1960s, oil exports began, bringing economic growth and funding major development across the emirates.

Formation of the UAE – 1971

On December 2, 1971, six emirates united to form the United Arab Emirates. Ras Al Khaimah joined a few months later, completing the federation of seven emirates under the leadership of Sheikh Zayed bin Sultan Al Nahyan.

Rapid Development and Global Rise

From the 1990s onward, the UAE expanded beyond oil. Dubai became a global business and tourism hub, while the country invested in education, innovation, and cultural preservation.
